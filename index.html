interesting facts about flying cicadas:

1. Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies, or nymphs. They are about the size of a grain of rice when they drop from a tree branch to the ground and start digging. 2. The loud whirring or buzzing sound you hear is an all-male cicada chorus. (Females make a much quieter sound by flicking their wings.) Cicadas don’t sing at night, so don’t blame them if you can’t sleep. 3. They’re true bugs (from the order Hemiptera). So you can call them a bug and be scientifically accurate.
